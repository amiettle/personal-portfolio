UX/UI Design

Food Waste Tracker

Summary

Food waste has been a focus among dining hall staff at UCI and the university has several efforts being made to increase sustainability but not enough is being done on the students’ end. Many students are unaware of the impact of their dining habits on the environment and tend to engage in wasteful practices. ‍ We designed a solution to encourage students to practice sustainability in their everyday lives by recording their food waste and becoming more mindful of their meals.

Details

Team

Amie L., Brian S., Ganesh R.

Scope

4 weeks (October 2021)

Tools

Figma, Miro

Research

To better understand the problem space, we created a survey and conducted interviews. The survey was sent out to UCI students and alumni to ensure that the potential differences between older students who have experience with the dining halls and newer students with less experience was accounted for. For more detailed insights, I wrote questions to interview dining hall staff about their own experiences with food waste at their workplaces.

Summary of findings:

  • We discovered that almost all students believed that reducing food waste at UCI was very important or at least somewhat.
  • Of those students, we found that many of them dine both on and off campus. This led us to believe that an ideal solution may entail decreasing food waste across a wider scope instead of exclusively "on-campus" dining settings.
  • However, we ultimately focused our solution on the context of on-campus dining because we found that students who dine at the dining halls reported higher food waste percentages.
  • In-depth interviews with two UCI dining hall staff revealed that the dining halls already have in place a few efforts to manage the issue on their end, but only so much can be done when students are sending back plates with piles of unfinished food.

When we performed a competitive analysis to evaluate existing solutions in the market, my team discovered that current trends included food logging systems, meal diaries, and inventory trackers. Strengths amongst our competitors were simple and clean designs and intuitive features that required little effort to learn. However, we found the food recognition AI to be rather inaccurate within our competitors, due to a lack of inclusion and diversity of food items. Lastly, we noticed that embedding social aspects or personalized goals motivates users to keep using the app and logging their food intake.

Ideation

After preparing a few sketches, we collectively gathered the key elements and features to be included in the wireframes. Taking these elements, we created an information architecture to serve as the structure of our app and guide our designs and wireframes.

Design

On the landing page we decided to display stats for personal tracking of dining practices and a leaderboard for comparing performance to friends. One of the main functionalities of the app is the meal logging feature. For logging meals, the user takes a picture of the meal before and after completion of the meal. The images are analyzed to get a rough estimate of how much food is left over from the meal. Completed meals are saved in a meal log history grouped by their date.

In order to encourage users to log their meals daily, we incorporated a community-like feature called ZotPack that allows users to compete with friends in their pack. Scores are determined by the amount of times a user logs their meals and the calculated food waste percentage. Additionally, daily check in on the app increases their scores, incentivizing consistent usage of the app.

Conclusion

My team aimed to solve the lack of awareness about on-going food waste concerns and its impact on the environment. Its presence on a university campus is especially significant due to the culture in on-campus dining halls and eateries. Some constraints that we had to keep in mind were that our users are busy students with countless responsibilities, therefore our solution must not impose a burden on their daily lives. A “nudge” in the right direction is what we aimed for our project to accomplish. If I were to expand on this design in the future, I would consider implementing reminders for notifying users to log their meals and designing an onboarding process to ensure a seamless user experience from start to finish.

Overall, I had lots of fun experimenting with new methods in this project and learning how to execute the design process.
